History to date

A few steps to date in the WWW project history are as follows:

  • March 1989
    CERN project proposal written and circulated for comment (TBL). Paper "HyperText and CERN" (in ASCII or WriteNow) produced as background.
  • October 1990
    Project proposal reformulated with encouragement from CN and ECP division management. RC is co-author.
  • November 1990
    Initial WorldWideWeb prototype developed on the NeXT (TBL).
  • November 1990
    Nicola Pellow joins and starts work on the line-mode browser. Bernd Pollermann helps get interface to CERNVM "FIND" index running. TBL gives a colloquium on hypertext in general.
  • December 1990
    First WWW and NeXTStep browsers demonstrable. Access is possible to hypertext files, CERNVM "FIND", and internet news articles.
  • February 1991
    Workplan for the purposes of ECP division.
  • 26 February 1991
    Presentation of the project to the ECP group.
  • March 1991
    Line mode browser (www) released to limited audience on priam vx, rs6000, sun4.
  • May 1991
    Workplan produced for CN/AS group
  • 17 May 1991
    Presentation to C5 committee. General release of WWW on central CERN machines.
  • 12 June 1991
    CERN Computer Seminar on WWW.
  • August 1991
    Files available on the net, posted on alt.hypertext, comp.sys.next, comp.text.sgml and comp.mail.multi-media. Jean-Francois Groff joins the project.
  • October 1991
    VMS/HELP and WAIS gateways installed. Mailing lists started. Anonymous telnet service started.
  • December 1991
    Poster presented and demo at HT91. CERN computer newsletter announces W3 to HEP world.
  • 15 January 1992
    Line mode browser release 1.1 available by anonymous FTP. See news. Presentation to AIHEP92 at La Londe.
  • 12 February 1992
    Line mode v 1.2 announced on alt.hypertext and other mailing lists.
  • March 1992
    Presentation and demo at JENC3 (Innsbruck). Carl Barker joins the project.
  • June 1992
    Presentation and demo at HEPVM (Lyon).
  • July 1992
    Distribution of WWW through CERNLIB including Viola.
  • August 1992
    Introduction of CVS for code management.
